# References

## Academic References

1. Brooks, R. A. (1991). Intelligence without representation. *Artificial Intelligence*, 47(1-3), 139-159.
   - Foundational paper on embodied cognition and the importance of physical interaction for intelligence.

2. Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). *How the body shapes the way we think: A new view of intelligence*. MIT Press.
   - Comprehensive exploration of how embodiment affects intelligence and learning.

3. Rus, D., & Tolley, M. T. (2015). Design, fabrication and control of soft robots. *Nature*, 521(7553), 467-475.
   - Important work on physical AI systems that interact with the world in novel ways.

4. Levine, S., Pastor, P., Krizhevsky, A., & Quillen, D. (2016). Learning hand-eye coordination for robotic grasping with deep learning and large-scale data collection. *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, 37(4-5), 421-436.
   - Example of Physical AI using deep learning for real-world manipulation.

5. Sadeghi, F., & Levine, S. (2017). CAD2RL: Real single-image flight without a single real image. *Proceedings of the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation*, 1218-1225.
   - Approach to bridging sim-to-real gap using domain randomization.
